	<img src="Planner Hats.png" alt="Planner Hats Logo">
	<h2 style="font-size:150%;"> The Planner Hats: Honeyeh Yazdizadeh, Hosna Hamdieh. Adrian Bolesnikov, Tina Yazdizadeh, Samantha Sieklicki </h2>
	<p style="font-size:150%;"> Quick look at the problem: Event planning is a hard and time consuming process and it would be a waste if not enough people show up. To make sure you are on the right path it is vital to both know your potential competitors and the times they are holding their events. Finding the perfect time might not be possible without an overall undrestanding of the domain's active players (groups) or popular event times and days.</p>
	<p style="font-size:150%;"> Our aim: Helping event planners to findout about other events, groups holding them and when they can have their own events.</p>
<h2 style="color:blue;font-size:200%;">Overview: </h2>
	    <p style="font-size:150%;">In this part active (hodling events) groups in the meta category selected will be shown, giving a general idea about who the main competitors could be. Parameters taken into consideration are as fallowed: </p>
	    <p style="font-size:150%;">The groups' member count</p>
	    <p style="font-size:150%;">The groups' average yes RSVP count (the average event attendance)</p>
	    <p style="font-size:150%;">The number of events held by the groups</p>
	    <p style="font-size:150%;">Note: There are filters facilitating personalization like selecting month, meta category, ect, so users are encouraged to take advantage of this filters to see what is most relevent to them. </p>
	<p style="font-size:150%;"> To give an overall view of popular times and days, the two other line graphs are presented. The graphs can be changed by going to the top of vertical heading and clicking on the small diagram poping up, to see either sorted bar charts going from more to less or less to more. </p>
